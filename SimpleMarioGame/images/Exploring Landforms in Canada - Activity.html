Introduction: Landforms in Canada

A landform is a natural feature on the surface of the earth. They can include large-scale features such as plains, plateaus, lakes, mountains, hills, valleys, and moraines among other things. In Canada, landform regions (also known as physiographic regions) are made up of diverse landscapes that contain a set of unique physical features.  

In this activity, you will explore some of the landforms that make up Canada, and how they affect where people live. You will also have an opportunity to test your knowledge of different types of landforms. 

You do not have to sign into ArcGIS Online to do this activity.

ASK AND EXPLORE

Q. How many different landforms can you think of that make up Canada’s physical geography?

Let's explore the location of different landforms in Canada. Use the spatial bookmarks to test your knowledge of some well-known landforms in Canada. 

  • Click on the Bookmarks button and select the first bookmark in the list.
  • Click on the Content button
    to view the data layers available to add to your map. 
  • You can uncheck the World Shaded Relief layer to view the map as an image basemap below. Depending on the landform you are exploring, you may want to activate the World Shaded Relief layer again.
  • Go through each bookmark to view different landforms in Canada.
  • Q: How many of these areas were you able to name? (Answers found on the last slide in the References and Resources section)
  • Locate three (3) additional landforms that you are familiar within Canada.

AQUIRE

Access data layers to explore landform regions in Canada.

  • Click on the Content button
    to view the data layers.
  • Click on the Canada’s Physiographic Regions layer.
  • Click on the Legend 
    and explore the map by panning and zooming in and out.
  • Q: What is the largest physiographic region in Canada? Q: What type of landforms can be found there?
  • Click the Content button
    and add the Canada Outline layer to the map.
  • Zoom into your province or territory to find out which physiographic region(s) encompass it.
  • Q: What are some of the prominent landforms (such as, mountain, plateau, drumlins) in your region? What is the spatial significance of this landform in your area (how do humans use it)?
